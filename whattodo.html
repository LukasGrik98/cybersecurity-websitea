What To Do If Something Goes Wrong

Sometimes online problems still happen, even if you try to be careful. This page explains what to do in different situations so you can react safely and quickly.

If you click a suspicious link

Close the page straight away and do not type in any personal information. If you are not sure, ask a teacher, parent, or trusted adult for help.

If you get a strange email

Do not reply and do not open any unknown attachments. Delete the email or report it as spam.

If your account gets hacked

Change your password immediately and log out of other devices if possible. Tell a teacher, parent, or school IT staff.

If a website asks for private information

Stop and check if the website is real before typing anything. Never share passwords, phone numbers, or addresses on suspicious websites.

If something feels unsafe

Trust your instincts and do not continue. It is always better to stop and ask someone for help.
